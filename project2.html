Problem

Energy forms the basic prerequisite for any developing economy. Despite having a geographical and geological advantage, India imports about 45% of its total energy requirements in form of conventional energy fuels, in addition, due to infrastructural bottle-neck its accessibility and affordability to the lowest level have been a chronic issue and an impediment to the rural development. Through this initiative, we plan to formulate a decentralised energy security strategy following a regional approach. Long term goal is to achieve self-sufficiency in Energy availability, utilising indigenous resources.

Aim

  1. Quality Education – lifelong learning and opportunities for all (SDG- 4)
  2. Gender Equality (SDG- 5)
  3. Skill demand match for decent work availability and economic growth (SDG- 8)
  4. Channelizing Demographic Dividend

Objectives

  1. Incorporating missing component in Quality Education and providing holistic training.
  2. Forecasting future job demands and preparing students at par with global standards.
  3. Exposing and disseminating information about variety of opportunities available abroad
  4. Sensitizing them towards India’s need and guiding with relevant academic and professional choices.
  5. Industry-academia linkages at higher secondary level.

Why fund us?

Initially mostly targeting CSR funds, CSR fundings as per government statistics have been mostly skewed – disproportionately high towards education and health without yielding any substantial output. By supporting our initiative, the company would be provided with a holistic development plan peculiar to a local region, it would help the industry in boosting the efficiency of their CSR activity and publicizing the same.
